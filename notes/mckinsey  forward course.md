# Adaptability And Resilience course 
"adaptability is not a trait, it is a state."

adaptability and resilience have 3 drivers : 
1. active learning (intention learning not performance goal )
2. re wire your mindset 
3. have goals for your self and organization 

## Active learning (intention learning )
معناها انك قبل ما تقدم علي حاجة حدد الاول الهدف من الاقدام علي الحاجة دي 
### Set your Intentions 
- مش شرط تكون واضحة 100% ، ولكن علي الاقل تحطها في الاطار الصحيح فيه طريقيتين : 
(1)focus on the Journey (not outcome) 
- "علمني ازاي اصطاد مش تديني السمكة"
Example 
- "I want to learn how to play softball" right 
- "I want to win the softball league trophy" wrong 
(2)make the learning meaningful for your personal (link to meaning)
- شوف واقع بعد ما تتعلم المهارة او الشيء ده إزاي هتقدر تستفاد منه 
Example : 
- "If i learn how to fix a car, i can help my friend and family if they ever run into trouble " - right situation 
- "I don't get why i need to learn how to fix car , i will just take it to the repair shop when it needs fixing" - wrong situation
### Learning intentions vs performance goal 
learning intentions 
- تركز اكثر علي الاستمتاع بالرحلة ، و الهدف منها انك تتعلم المهارة بغض النظر عن النتيجة 
performance goals 
- اهداف بارقام الهدف تحقيقها ، و لكن ذلك عندما تكون عندك المهارة بالفعل .

- عادي يكون عندك اكتر من mindset تفلتر بيها الموقف الي قدامك و لكن ده متوقف علي الموقف نفسه الفكرة هنا انك تختار ال mindset الصحيحة في الموقف الصحيح.

## define the problem using tools 
### what is problem solving mean ? 
- the art of make decisions for ourselves or our team (is art and science ) 
- what is the 20% that's going to drive 80% of the value that's you're going to actually see ? 
### what is the problem solving approach ? 
1. actions : what steps do you take ? 
2. stakeholders  
3. approach : how to adjust for different sorts of problems ? 
4. toolkit : what is your resources ? 
5. strengths : what do you do well ? 
6. opportunities : how might you improve ? 

### what is the hallmark of great problem solvers ? 
- is leveraging the input of others 

### how much time do you have , and what you do in each time ? 
1. in the moment 
2. week or two weeks 
3. long term time 
- in the moment time : you don't have time  to actually formally whip out a sheet of paper , but you should focus on define what is the problem and have you align all the stakeholders and how the solution space we're looking for is scoped 
- week or two weeks : thinking about how do you prioritize the issues and the analysis you want to conduct
